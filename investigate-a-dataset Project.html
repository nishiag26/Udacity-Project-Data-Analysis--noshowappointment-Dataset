Introduction¶

This dataset collects information from 100k medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment. A number of characteristics about the patient are included in each row.
● ‘ScheduledDay’ tells us on what day the patient set up their appointment.
● ‘Neighborhood’ indicates the location of the hospital.
● ‘Scholarship’ indicates whether or not the patient is enrolled in Brasilian welfare program Bolsa Família.
● ‘No_show’ it says ‘No’ if the patient showed up to their appointment, and ‘Yes’ if they did not show up.

Conclusions¶

  1. Out of total patients: Patients showed up for the Appointment are 85307 and patients did not showed up are 21675.

  2. We can see that Brazilian Welfare Scholarship does not shows a positive trend on no-shows for the Appointment
    Percentage of patients with scholarship and showed up for appointment 76 %
    Percentage of patients without scholarship and showed up for appointment 80 %

  3. Out of total Old aged Patients 84% of the patients showed up for the appointment whereas 78.9% of the Adults showed up and 77.5% of children showed up.

4.Out of total patients who did not recieved the SMS 83% of patient showed up, whereas Out of total patients who recieved SMS only 72% of patient showed up.(This looks opposite to what usually happens. People who do receive a reminder SMS usually tend to visit the hospital more than those who haven't received an SMS. Or this could be an SMS that was recived while booking an appointment and could possibly have no correlation with the visit probability.)

5.Out of total patients, 70115 patients are female and 36867 patients are male.
Out of total females 79.6% showed up whereas out of total males 79.9% showed up.

  1. On the basis of the Awaited Days vs No. Of Patients Graph, its found that if the Appointment is of the same date as the Scheduled Date then out of the total patients 37154 patients show up for the appointment.

  2. As observed from the graphs of Hypertension,Diabetes and Alcoholism it can be concluded that out of the total hypertension patients, 82% of them showed up and out of total diabetes patients, 81.9% of them showed up and out of total people having alcoholism, 79.8% of them showed up for the appointment.

8.On the basis of the line graph of No. of Awaited Days for appointment and No. of Patients showed up, its known that more number of people showup for the appointment when they get the appointment for less number of awaiting days.

Limitations and Missing Information¶

Few limitations of dataset are:

  1. It would help in better analysis, if the time of the Appointment would also be given in the dataset.
  2. Data could be more appropriate as few ages were less than 0,Scheduled Date was before the Appointment Date.
  3. Information in Handicap Column could be more specific which would have been more useful for analysis.
